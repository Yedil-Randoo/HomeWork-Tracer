A comprehensive academic management system built by students, for students. Bridging the gap between messy notebooks and digital excellence.

01. Project Vision

HomeworkTracker was conceived as a response to the fragmented nature of current school platforms. While systems like Edupage focus on administrative reporting, students are left without a personal tool for self-management.

Our goal was to create a centralized "Academic OS" where a student can document every specific instruction, deadline, and resource in one place. By focusing on a minimalist UI, we eliminate "app fatigue" and allow users to focus on what matters: their studies.

The Problem

Modern students manage over 10 subjects simultaneously. Information is often lost across messengers, paper diaries, and verbal instructions. This leads to missed deadlines and unnecessary stress.

The Solution: A web-based hub that follows you from the school laptop to your home computer, ensuring constant access to your task list.

03. Full Sponsor Interview

What does your product consist of?
Our product will allow students to write down their assignments for the week or for a specific deadline themselves, meaning the subject, the task, and the deadline.
Is this application only for students?
Yes, students will use it primarily. However, it's flexible—adults can use it as a planner for work goals or additional courses. But our main focus remains school projects.
Why a website and not a native application?
A website is more accessible during school hours. Since phones are often restricted in classrooms, students can easily manage their tasks using school laptops and the web interface.
How will the tasks be sorted?
Currently, sorting is based on deadlines. We are taking feedback into account to implement goal-based sorting and a full monthly calendar view for better visual planning.
Can you mark tasks as completed?
Absolutely. We’ve implemented a status-tracking feature. Marking a task as "Done" is crucial for a student’s sense of progress and effective time management.
What about reminders and notifications?
That is an important feature we want to tackle next. While difficult to implement in the initial web version, we are researching ways to add push notifications for upcoming deadlines.
